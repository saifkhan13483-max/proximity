Frequently Asked Questions

We start by reviewing your credit reports from all three bureaus (Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion). Our specialists identify inaccurate, outdated, or unverifiable items. We then draft targeted dispute letters and send them to the bureaus. Under the FCRA, bureaus have 30 days to investigate. Successfully disputed items are removed from your report.

Most clients begin seeing results within 30–90 days of their first dispute cycle. The exact timeline depends on the complexity of your case, the number of items disputed, and how quickly the credit bureaus respond. We monitor all responses and continue working until every disputable item is resolved.

We cover all three major credit bureaus: Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion. Every dispute is filed with all three bureaus simultaneously, ensuring maximum coverage. Many lenders check all three, so it's essential to have accurate information on every report.

Your security is our top priority. We use bank-level encryption (AES-256) to protect all data in transit and at rest. We never sell or share your personal information with third parties. Our systems are regularly audited for compliance with federal data protection regulations.

A rejected dispute doesn't mean the fight is over. Our team analyzes the bureau's response and develops a refined strategy. We may request method of verification, escalate to the CFPB, send additional documentation, or pursue a legal route if the reporting is clearly inaccurate.
